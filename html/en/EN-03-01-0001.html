Smart Traffic Enforcement

Applying TOPES’ excellent detection technology, TOPCAM2016 detects any objects (vehicles) violating traffic rules
and relays the relevant images/videos to the center.

Speed-Enforcement System
Redlight-Enforcement System
Point to Point Speed Enforcement System

This system detects a vehicle’s speed and possible signal violations using detective sensors (Loop, Laser, Radar) and cameras installed on the road. The Point to Point Speed Enforcement
System detects violations in a car’s average speed. It then sends out the vehicle’s information (vehicle number, time of passage, speed, and vehicle image) related to the violation, which is
gathered at the jurisdiction center through the TOPES controller, serving as the basis for issuing a ticket to the driver in question.

Two-Wheeled Vehicle’s Speed
Traffic-Signal Control

This system detects violations of traffic laws in terms of traffic-signal violations, not wearing a helmet, and driving on areas reserved for pedestrians, and this is carried out using detective sensors (Loop, Laser, Radar) and cameras installed on the road.

Illegal Parking Enforcement System

This system detects vehicles within a specific area using a camera. The system is installed in specific areas that prohibit stopping or parking, and, in the case of a violation, all information for the concerned vehicle (vehicle number, violation duration) is transmitted to the center.

Tailgating-Control System

This system can be set to track tailgating at a busy intersection to record a vehicle stuck in the intersection as the light turns to green, resulting in blocked traffic, and it saves the video to include the license plate number and other information for tracking.

Intelligent Traffic Management

TOPES’ exclusive customized solutions offer optimized resolutions for various traffic problems,
ranging from traffic information collected on the road to detailed specifications on the vehicles extracted.

Traffic-Safety Integrated System

This system enhances traffic safety and management through detective CCTV cameras, weather-measuring sensors, and vehicle detectors installed on national highways, expressways,
at both ends of tunnels, blind spots, parking lots at highway service areas, danger areas with falling rocks and soil, rest stops along the highway, etc. by remotely controlling far-off traffic circumstances to check on traffic and weather-related information for active responses to the given traffic conditions. A special feature of this system includes the user’s selective choice of modules to install and operate for use in linkage with various weather sensors and warning-display devices.

Automatic Vehicle Identification System

This system uses a device that detects vehicles running across multiple lanes through high-definition video. This system can be installed on any type of road and sends all related information to the center, including the vehicle number, time of passage, speed, and an image of the vehicle.

Old-Diesel-Vehicle Control System

This system uses pre-installed detective sensors (Loop, Laser, Radar) and cameras to detect vehicles before sending the detected information (vehicle number, vehicle image, etc.) to the center.

Smart Road Surface Management System

This system aims to prevent traffic accidents and fatal accidents by forecasting road-surface conditions, such as freezing and blow-ups due to weather changes.

Detailed Specification-Extraction System

Overcoming the drawbacks of AVC (Automatic Vehicle Classification), this system provides non-buried simplified construction, easy reinstallation, and maintenance by developing a new kind of AVC equipment that meets the cutting-edge advancements in the content of road-traffic statistics. Based on AI recognition technology, the system provides detailed vehicle-classification information for 12 vehicle types, along with intelligent vehicle-classification information, such as the specified type (taxi, compact car, etc.) and electric cars.

Axle-Manipulative Overloaded-Vehicle Control System

When controlling overloads at the tollgate, an overloaded vehicle with a lift axle can avoid checking in for weight measurement by unloading its axle. To prevent this occurrence, this system utilizes the front and side video-recognition technology of the axle-manipulative DB-detection system to monitor overloaded vehicles and automatically classify axle-manipulative vehicles.

Video Content

This system provides not only videos for news, sports, weather forecasts, advertisements, and local information but also various genres of audio, such as music for all passengers on high-speed railroads, through LCD monitors and wireless earphones.
